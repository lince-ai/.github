# üí° What is Lince

Lince is a digital tool for two types of registration:

### 1.¬†**What you need**.

People have different needs, each will need to register specific items and services, these can be:

- Food, Tools, Clothing, Toys, Furniture....
- Light, Water, Sanitation...
- Gardening Services, Technical Support in a certain area, Transport...

The purpose of the registration is to minimize unmet needs because of a lack of quality information about them. Each registration will be a vote for investment in a solution. This information can be shared publicly, to NGOs, governments, companies and individuals, or privately and encrypted (E2E), to family and acquaintances.

### 2 .¬†**What you can deliver.**

As individuals, we can give away items that we own and no longer need, children's toys, older blankets, clothes that don't fit, food that will go to waste, etc. But up to a limit.

Organizations often start with the goal of delivering a product or service to people, but do not have the necessary information about those they want to help. You can post on Lince the services and products your organization offers, so that those who seek them can find them more easily.

# üìà Impact

With this data, you can receive information about your population, with a real-time needs census model. There will be interactions between individuals, donations to organizations to distribute resources, and the offering of existing products and services through a platform that facilitates the process.

The mapping out of needs facilitates a planned economy that accounts for those needs and degrows when not needed. Much is seen in respect for the ecological impact that endless growth causes on the planet. Items will be destined to the homes of those in need, they will not become garbage, they will be treated as donations or raw materials. There will be fewer items in landfills, less pollution, and less illegal dumping. Lince is a tool for the creation and exchange of information. The power and responsibility to change the real world with freight and delivery of any product or service will remain in the hands of people.

The flow of needs and deliveries generates a donation economy, feeding a support network for all. Non-Profit Organizations, NGOs, Governments and communities are all sources of social support, products and services, and they all have costs. With Lince, individuals can lighten this demand, allowing organizations to invest in other actions.

Little positive interactions can be created with enough security and information. Example: you need to walk a certain distance, it might not be too long that you want to call a driver, but not so short that you wont take some time. By putting that need into Lince, any other passerby Lince user that owns a car, that you allow to see, will be able to help. Giving rides is not something they do for a living but your destination was on their way to work. They stop for 5 seconds and you save 15 minutes. As with real world paid car rides, security is a top concern. With any sort of personal or organizational interactions many measures will be taken to protect both parties information and well-being.

This tool creates the possibility for companies or governments to plan the delivery of products and services. Producer organizations, inserted in a supply chain, will have more information to not fail to meet certain demands or overproduce. Very often a "whiplash effect" is observed with producers being warned too late of the lack or surplus of demand, failing to meet needs or overproducing and losing products by organic or marketing expiration dates. With a significant number of users and their inputs, companies will see the need to reduce then redundant demands forecasting and customer relationship sectors.

# üèõ Organization

The organization will follow a Non-Profit model, receiving donations and grants from organizations that wish to fund the project for their own use (e.g. Linux Foundation and its sponsors). As a Free Software initiative, governments and communities are able to utilize Lince as a means for best investing in social support. There is the possibility of running Lince on their own servers and also funding the project that programs, updates and maintains it.

# üíª Technological Infrastructure

By making the source code freely available we enable improvement and ownership of the software by anyone with interest. We consider the possibility of hosting a server for POC and performing donation cycles among acquaintances. We are also studying the feasibility of running servers with the ActivityPub protocol to connect Lince's instances to the Fediverse (think of accounts created in servers that can connect to any other, similar to email, not blockchain).

The best ways to sign up will be through website and app. However, since not all people have access to technology, other ways of filling out will prove necessary. It is possible that in social support centers like Brazil's SUS Health Centers people in critical situations can be registered.
